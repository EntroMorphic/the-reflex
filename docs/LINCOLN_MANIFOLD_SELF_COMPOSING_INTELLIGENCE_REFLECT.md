## Unpacking

In traditional computing:
- Hardware is fixed (transistors, wires)
- Software is flexible (instructions, data)
- The programmer writes the software

In neural networks:
- Topology is fixed (layers, connections)
- Weights are flexible (learned values)
- The training process adjusts the weights

In FPGA:
- Resources are fixed (LUTs, registers)
- Configuration is flexible (but static after load)
- The designer specifies the configuration

In **echip**:
- Shapes are fixed (once crystallized)
- Routes are flexible (continuously learning)
- Configuration emerges from OPERATION

The chip configures itself. Not during a training phase. Not during a configuration step. **Continuously, through use.**

## The Three Levels of Self-Modification

### Level 1: Weight Plasticity (Hebbian)
Routes change their strength based on correlated activity. This is like synaptic plasticity in the brain. Fast (every tick). Reversible. The fundamental learning mechanism.

### Level 2: Topological Plasticity (Pruning)
Routes that aren't used dissolve. Connections disappear. The topology simplifies. This is like synaptic pruning during development. Slower (takes many ticks). Irreversible (route is gone). The efficiency mechanism.

### Level 3: Structural Plasticity (Crystallization)
New shapes emerge from void. New computational elements appear. The chip grows. This is like neurogenesis. Slowest (requires sustained entropy). Permanent (until dissolved). The growth mechanism.

Traditional systems have Level 0: no self-modification.
Neural networks have Level 1: weight changes only.
**echip has all three levels simultaneously.**

## The Developmental Perspective

A human brain:
- Starts with ~100 billion neurons
- Forms ~100 trillion synapses
- Prunes to ~100 trillion synapses (different ones)
- Loses neurons over time (but can generate some new ones)

The brain doesn't just learn. It **develops**. Structure and function co-evolve.

echip does the same:
- Starts with initial shapes (seed structure)
- Forms routes through crystallization and explicit creation
- Prunes unused routes
- Dissolves unused shapes
- Crystallizes new shapes from void

This isn't learning in the ML sense. This is **ontogeny**—the development of an individual from a seed.

- Discover efficient circuits for its input patterns

- Trade off resources between tasks
- Repair itself after damage

These are properties of **adaptive systems**, not just computational systems.

## The Philosophical Perspective

Is echip intelligent?

Intelligence requires:
- **Sensing**: echip has inputs ✓
- **Acting**: echip has outputs ✓
- **Learning**: echip has Hebbian plasticity ✓
- **Memory**: echip has latches, weights ✓
- **Adaptation**: echip has pruning, crystallization ✓
- **Goals**: echip has... ?

The missing piece is goals. echip adapts, but to what end?

Perhaps the goal is implicit: **survive**. Routes that contribute to output survive. Routes that don't, die. Shapes that participate in useful computation persist. Shapes that don't, dissolve.

This is a selection pressure. This is evolution. This is... a form of wanting.
