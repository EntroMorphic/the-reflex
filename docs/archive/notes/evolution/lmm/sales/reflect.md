# Reflect: Sales & Deployment

## Core Insight

**We're selling confidence, not software.**

The customer isn't buying code. They're buying:
- Confidence their robot won't hurt someone
- Confidence their system won't fail
- Confidence they can achieve what competitors can't

Price reflects confidence delivered, not lines of code written.

---

## Resolved Tensions

### Node 2 vs Node 3 (Free vs Paid)
**Resolution:** Clear value boundary.

Open Core = "Can you do it?"
Commercial = "Can you do it well, reliably, at scale?"
Premium = "Can you do it certifiably, in safety-critical contexts?"

The free tier proves capability. The paid tiers deliver confidence.

### Node 9 (No Discounting)
**Resolution:** Discounting is a signal problem.

If we discount, we signal:
- Our prices were inflated
- Our value is negotiable
- We're desperate for the deal

Instead, offer:
- Extended pilot period
- Additional training
- Enhanced support

Same revenue, different framing. Value-add, not price-cut.

### Node 11 (International Pricing)
**Resolution:** Single global price with currency adjustment.

Same USD equivalent worldwide. Converts to local currency. No regional discounts.

Why: Simpler, fairer, prevents arbitrage.

## The Conversion Funnel

```
Open Source User
      │
      ↓ (needs scale, support, or advanced features)
      
Commercial Prospect
      │
      ↓ (readiness assessment)
      
Qualified Lead
      │
      ↓ (pilot program)
      
Pilot Customer
      │
      ↓ (success metrics achieved)
      
Production Customer
      │
      ↓ (certification needs or custom requirements)
      
Premium Customer
```

Each stage has clear entry/exit criteria. No ambiguity.

## Pricing Psychology

| Tier | Price | Anchoring |
|------|-------|-----------|
| Open Core | $0 | "It's free to start" |
| Commercial | $5K/mo | "Less than one engineer's time" |
| Premium | $200K | "Less than one safety incident" |

Always anchor against larger costs. We're cheap compared to:
- Engineers (Commercial)
- Incidents (Premium)
- Competitors (Both)

## What I Now Understand

Sales is about matching customers to tiers, not convincing customers to buy.

- Open Core users who need scale → Commercial
- Commercial users who need certification → Premium
- Anyone who doesn't fit → Not our customer

The sales process is FILTER then SERVE. Not CONVINCE then SELL.

Pricing should feel like a bargain relative to alternatives. "This costs less than one safety incident" makes $200K feel cheap.
