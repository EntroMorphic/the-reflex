## The Three Components

1. **Frozen Shapes (the nouns)**: Static computational elements. NAND, LATCH, ADD, NEURON. They do what they do. They don't change.

2. **Mutable Routes (the verbs)**: Dynamic connections. They carry signals. They have weights. They strengthen with use. They weaken with neglect. They dissolve back to void.

3. **Entropy Field (the grammar)**: The space between. Tracks activity. Accumulates silence. When entropy goes critical, new shapes crystallize.

Together: a self-composing intelligence.

## Hebbian Learning

"Neurons that fire together wire together."

Routes that carry correlated signals strengthen. The chip learns associations. Not through backpropagation. Not through gradient descent. Through USE.

This is how the brain learns. This is how habits form. This is how skills develop.

---

## Pruning

Use it or lose it.

Routes that don't carry signals weaken. Eventually they dissolve. Return to void. Free up capacity for routes that matter.

This is why you forget unused languages. This is why skills fade. This is how the brain stays efficient.

## Crystallization

The brain can't grow new neurons (mostly). But echip can.

When void accumulates enough entropy—when there's been enough silence in a region—it crystallizes into a new shape. The chip grows.

What shape? Depends on the local gradients. High gradient (lots of nearby activity) = routing shape (BUFFER). Horizontal flow = logic shape (NAND). Vertical flow = memory shape (LATCH).

The environment shapes the structure.
