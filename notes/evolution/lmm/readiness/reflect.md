# Reflect: Readiness Assessment

## Core Insight

**The assessment is the product.**

We're not selling software to anyone with a credit card. We're partnering with organizations ready to succeed. The assessment proves we care about outcomes, not just sales.

This DIFFERENTIATES us. Competitors will sell to anyone. We qualify first. That builds trust.

---

## Resolved Tensions

### Node 8 (Turning Away Customers)
**Resolution:** Turning away unready customers PROTECTS the business.
- Failed deployments damage reputation
- Support burden drains resources
- Unhappy customers become anti-references

Short-term revenue < long-term reputation.

### Node 11 (Minimum Viable Readiness)
**Resolution:** For PILOT: 12/25 with clear remediation plan.
For PRODUCTION: 15/25 minimum.

Pilots are learning opportunities. Production is commitment.

### Node 9 (Gap Remediation)
**Resolution:** Offer three paths:
1. **Self-remediation:** We tell you what to fix, you fix it.
2. **Co-remediation:** We work together to close gaps.
3. **Defer:** Come back when ready.

We're consultative, not gatekeeping.

## The Five Pillars Weighted

Not all pillars are equal:

| Pillar | Weight | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Hardware | 20% | Can be fixed with money |
| Software | 20% | Can be fixed with engineering |
| Operational | 25% | Requires domain knowledge |
| Team | 20% | Can be hired or trained |
| Organizational | 15% | Hardest to change |

**Operational is the linchpin.** If they don't understand their own system, the Forge produces garbage.

---

## The Domain Expert Deep Dive

Questions to extract tacit knowledge:
1. "What does normal sound like? Feel like? Look like?"
2. "What happens right before failures?"
3. "What would you do if you were the robot?"
4. "What patterns do you notice that you've never documented?"
5. "What do new operators get wrong?"

The Domain Expert might not know they know. Our job is to help them articulate.

---

## What I Now Understand

The readiness assessment is STRATEGIC, not just tactical. It:
1. Qualifies customers (saves resources)
2. Builds trust (we care about success)
3. Extracts knowledge (improves the Forge)
4. Sets expectations (realistic timelines)
5. Creates urgency (gaps need closing)

The assessment process itself is valuable. Customers learn about their own systems. We learn about their environment. Even if they don't buy, the assessment was worthwhile.
